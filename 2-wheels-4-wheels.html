In the last few years, I’ve seen major shift toward 4-wheel suitcases, everywhere. Well, almost everywhere.

I’ve never seen a flight attendant with a spinner suitcase. In fact, it was challenging to find an image of a flight attendant with a spinner suitcase on the internet. Here is a search in Google images.

I think the reason for that is spinner suitcases are simply better for most people, but maybe not for frequent travellers and a few other exceptions. Read on as this is probably one of the biggest differences between suitcases, along with the debate around soft versus hard shells.

4 wheels suitcases

Spinners have become extremely popular. And I can see why -- you must have seen how effortlessly people are spinning their 4-wheel suitcases around the airport. But there is more:

  • Less effort. A lot less effort is required from you to move a spinner most of the time, unless it is a rough surface.
  • More balance. Spinners don’t fall over and can stand upright.
  • Ease of use. You can push a spinner in front of you, or by your side,or behind you. And it’s a lot easier to travel with more than one suitcase. I repeat, a lot easier.

All of this comes at a small cost, however:

  • Quality. Spinners tend to break a lot more easily, particularly the low quality ones, so if you are a frequent flyer you need to buy a higher end suitcase or you will soon regret it. So a good one is probably going to be more expensive than the 2-wheel alternative.
  • Space. For carry-ons, spinners offer less internal packing space and take up more room in the overhead compartment.

2 wheels suitcases

As you have probably already figured out, 2 wheel suitcases are more sturdy and tend to break less. So they are better suited for frequent travellers or people that will use them on a rough surface. Check out the Travelpro FlightCrew range, most of which is on 2 wheels.

The downside of 2 wheel suitcases is that in most situations they require more effort from you. And they can tip forward, but this typically isn’t a big issue.

What you should buy

Unless you’re a very frequent traveller (think flight attendant) or you’re planning to use your suitcase on a dirt road and/or the beautiful cobblestone roads of Rome, do yourself a favour and buy a spinner suitcase.
