I know what I’m talking about

When I buy items for me (not just travel stuff) I spend a whole bunch of time researching the best options out there, sometimes carefully reading tens of customer reviews. It would be fair to call me a geek, no doubt.

What's the point of all that, you’d ask. I am obsessed with value for money. I hate spending money on crappy stuff.

The question is, have you got the spare time to do the research?

Good news. I’ve done it for you. I’ve invested the same amount of time and energy in choosing the picks and recommendations on the website as I would do for my own stuff.

How I do it

For each article, I start by applying basic filter criteria on the thousands of options on Amazon. I then create a database (remember, I’m a geek) with all customer reviews (often times 1,000+ of reviews) and use some cool algorithms for text summarisation and sentiment analysis to distinguish the good from the bad.

This is the easy part.

I am then left with about 100 products, which I manually analyse to narrow down the list.

The reason this works is because using real reviews from paying customers is simply the best way to assess the worth of a product. Standard product descriptions from sellers and manufacturers can be biased and misleading. The best products are those which have a product description, which perfectly matches customer reviews.

On the flip side, claims in product reviews, such as "we use the best materials", are easily disproved with a few customer reviews showing that a bag didn't last very long.
