Suitcases

10 hand luggage brands that will make you enjoy your travels

If you are struggling to pick a suitcase brand, you're not alone. There are too many brands that appear to be similar and yet they are slightly different. Different enough to make the decision a difficult one.

Alex
Luggage black belt

Introduction

There are many hand luggage brands out there, but there aren't that many that offer a good balance of price and quality. And brand reputation, of course.

In this review, I am purpusefully limiting the mentions to the top 10 hand luggage brands of 2017. I don't want to overwhelm you with choice, but at the same time I want to cover all price ranges and luggage types - soft, hard, and even smart luggage. For those who are interested, this is how I researched the brands.

Without any further ado, here's a brief summary of what each branch has to offer.

The brand list

  • Travelpro

Samsonite

Rating: 4.4 / 5
High quality

Samsonite is perhaps the world's most well known luggage brand. And there is a reason for that -- Samsonite suitcases look great and are very durable and functional, but will not cost you a small fortune.

American Tourister

Rating: 4.6 / 5
Well made

American Tourister is another great brand, which is in fact owned by Samsonite. The biggest difference to me is the incredibly vibrant colours of the suitcases -- a pure joy to look at them. Ah, and they are also cheaper!

Briggs and Riley

Rating: 4.8 / 5
Top quality

If you are prepared to pay top dollar, Briggs and Riley might be the best brand for you -- insane durability, great designs, and a lifetime warranty, which can only speak for the extremely high quality of the suitcases.

Travelpro

Rating: 4.7 / 5
Frequent flyers

In 1987, the founder of Travelpro designed the carry-on suitcase as you know it today. Today, Travelpro suitcases are the choice of many flight attendants -- if you are a frequent flyer, you probably won’t go wrong.

Bluesmart

Rating: 4.5 / 5
Connected

If you’re into connected devices, Bluesmart suitcases will most likely meet all of your needs -- suitcases that charge your phone, can (almost) never get lost, and weigh themselves. Geeky, but super handy.

Delsey

Rating: 4.6 / 5
Beautiful designs

If I have to think of one brand that offers beautiful designs at a reasonable price, surely that is Delsey. Its unmistakably French designs are elegant, unconventional, and instantly recognisable. Those of you who want to keep up with their image while travelling, buy a Delsey suitcase and look no further.

Aerolite

Rating: 4.2 / 5
Cheap

Very lightweight and cheap, Aerolite suitcase are great for most travellers. Whilst there are some quality issues, none of these are deal breakers, so if you’re looking for a suitcase that won’t break your bank and do a decent job, this is it.

Antler

Rating: 4.2 / 5
British

A UK luggage brand with a worldwide reputation, Antler has been in business for over 100 years -- go for this if you’d like to get proven quality and support a British brand.

Tumi

Rating: 4.7 / 5
Functional superiority

Tumi suitcases are designed to make your life and travels easier -- passing through security, carrying multiple bags with one hand, preventing shoulder straps slipping, and lots more. These suitcases are functionally superior but are also priced accordingly. Well worth.

Victorinox

Rating: 4.1 / 5
Swiss quality

Inspired by the iconic Swiss army knife, Victorinox suitcases are packed with functions, both for business and adventure -- start here if you value versatility more than storage space.
