What makes a great carry-on suitcase

The single most important thing is that your carry-on suitcase must fit your airline's hand luggage dimensions or you may need to check it in and pay extra. But what makes a great suitcase? To answer this question, we went through hundreds of products reviews and the analysis revealed that the following features separate a good suitcase from a great one:

  • Lightweight is not merely a nice to have feature but an essential attribute due to airline hand luggage weight limitations.
  • Numerical combination locks can provide extra security and peace of mind for your valuables.
  • Suitcases with 4-wheels are easier to roll in any direction.
  • If you are a frequent flyer, durability is the single most important feature to look for.

Things to watch out for

The analysis also revealed the things to watch out for when selecting your hand luggage. Whilst none of these are deal-breakers, they are all things that can easily be avoided by carefully selecting your suitcase.

  • Some very lightweight suitcases cannot stand upright, unless leaned against a wall.
  • Lower quality suitcases are more likely to get cosmetic damage, or even get their fabric ripped.
  • Spinner versions of suitcases (4-wheels) may have less internal space than two-wheeled equivalents.
  • Hard-shell suitcases typically lack external pockets and have less packing space.
